### What is a RFC?

A **Request For Comments** is a formal document usually published by the Internet Engineering Task Force(IEFT), that details
technical rules, specifications, protocols for the internet. Desipte the name of request a published RFC is a finalised standarded
**locked in place** rather than a draft.

They are published to create a permanent, open record of how different network protocols, like email, Wi-Fi, 
or web browsing should function. Without RFCs, a smartphone
in London might not be able to communicate with a server in Tokyo because their software wouldn't be speaking the same technical language.
This **interoperability** of different devices is one of many benefits of using RFCs.
